Sessions

Provisional programme

The one-day conference programme will have multiple tracks focused on different disciplines. Next to plenary and track sessions, there will be plenty of networking opportunities, and possibilities to exchange knowledge and ideas with other attendees. An informal dinner will be organised for all attendees interested.

Based on our Dutch User Conference, we have already prepared a basic programme containing the sessions mentioned below. Please note that more sessions and information will be added over the next weeks, so be sure to check back regularly for updates.

Call for sessions

Since the User Conference is aimed at the MyTimetable users, we invite you to send proposals for presentations, workshops, breakout sessions, and discussions to complete the programme. These can be sessions you want to present or chair yourself, or sessions you would like Eveoh to prepare.

Our current sessions are mostly aimed at functional and technical application managers. We would be very happy to add sessions at a strategic and business level as well.

Please submit your proposals to Marco Krikke at marco@eveoh.nl.

Keynote - Over the Horizon: Calendaring Futures in UK Higher Education

Leon Smith Director of Registry Services, Manchester Metropolitan University

MyTimetable 3.0

MyTimetable 3.0 offers the long-awaited integration with Microsoft Office 365, Microsoft Exchange and Google Apps for Education. We will demonstrate the integration and its advantages. Also, we will showcase other new features, such as "selective sync", a new help page, room displays and a room availability app.

strategic functional

Distance learning with Pexip, Syllabus Plus and MyTimetable

Pexip offers a platform that connects existing communication tools, such as Microsoft Skype for Business, browsers, and traditional videoconferencing equipment. Together with Pexip, we will show how institutions can benefit from the integration of Pexip Infinity, Syllabus Plus, and MyTimetable, to enable blended learning, distance learning and personal meeting spaces.

strategic functional

Workshop: MyTimetable roadmap

We will discuss our current MyTimetable roadmap and invite you to provide us with more information on your requirements.

functional

Smart scheduling in the cloud

strategic functional

Tony Vicente Business Development Manager, Semestry Ltd

In-depth: technical management of MyTimetable

In this session, we will introduce the different components of MyTimetable 3.0. We will talk about technical application management of MyTimetable, including monitoring the application, incident analysis and common maintenance tasks. Also, we invite you to express your wishes regarding technical application management. Finally, the technical implications of the Microsoft Office 365, Microsoft Exchange and Google Apps for Education integration are presented.

technical

Attendance monitoring and MyTimetable

In this session, we will present the possibilities of attendance registration in MyTimetable and the integration we offer with Simac Presto. Discussed will be the possibilities for attendance registration by staff, self-registration by students, presenting the admission status of activities, feedback on attendance registrations, and using personalised timetables as a source for attendance monitoring.

strategic functional

Integrating MyTimetable with your SIS or DLE

MyTimetable offers integrations with several Student Information Systems and Digital Learning Environments. This is used to offer a personalised timetable using enrolments data from these systems. We will showcase some integrations and discuss possible other integrations.

technical

In-depths: less known features of MyTimetable

In this session, less known features of MyTimetable are showcased, that might have added value for your publication of timetables. We will show, among other things, draft timetables and publishing timetables in different stages.

functional

Timetable data sources: what are the possibilities?

MyTimetable is able to read timetabling data from different data sources. We will showcase some solutions that are currently in use with our customers, such as displaying deadlines in MyTimetable and displaying extracurricular events in the student's timetable. Also, we will discuss a transition scenario to other timetabling systems, and what the consequences of this are for the students and staff members.
