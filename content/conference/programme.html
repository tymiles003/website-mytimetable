Keynote – Over the Horizon: Calendaring Futures in UK Higher Education

9:45 – 10:45 / Committee Room 1

This session will explore the current and future challenges and opportunities facing UK Higher Education in terms of calendaring services offered to students and staff.

In order to achieve this, it will offer a retrospective review of the historical development of these services and related timetable design and delivery methodologies. This will seek to illustrate the key drivers for service delivery before analysing how these have changed, expanded or remained static during the past twenty years, before applying these to future options.

The review will cover process, organisation and technology, and it will be through these areas that future possibilities will be examined. These possibilities will be contextualised by issues around data sourcing and integration, and include topics such as individual customisation, interactivity, functional expansion and new delivery options.

The purpose of the session is to provoke thought and discussion, rather than an idealised blue print for the future, as higher education professionals and our external partners have always delivered the best solutions when working collaboratively.

Attendance monitoring and registration, the do's and don'ts

13:00 – 14:00 / Seminar Room -1.62

In this session, Simac Education will present several possibilities of Simac Presto for attendance registration, engagement monitoring and reporting. This includes access control to enforce a door policy, for example for exams that have an entry requirement. Presto's rule-based early warning system supports the institution in monitoring student attendance and engagement. It supports following up with students when necessary, including keeping track of a detailed document flow.

A possible integration with MyTimetable will be introduced as well. Using MyTimetable, students can self-register their attendance, view their registered attendance, and view if they are authorised to access activities such as exams.
